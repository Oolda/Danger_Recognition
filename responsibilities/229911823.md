### My Responsibilities

My primary contributions to this project spanned both versions of the system development, the ablation study, and the final presentation.

In the **first version**, I was responsible for designing and implementing the temporal modeling component. I built the initial LSTM Autoencoder pipeline, which at that time treated the LSTM and the autoencoder as separate sequential components. I ran the full training and evaluation pipeline and shared the results with my teammate so they could generate the ground truth labels from the XML annotation files and perform the initial comparison. Although this version produced results, we later identified that the root cause of the poor performance was in the spatial feature extraction stage — the CNN encoder was not pretrained, meaning the 128-dimensional feature vectors fed into the LSTM were essentially random noise. This made the first version unreliable and required us to rebuild the pipeline from scratch.

In the **second version**, after my teammate fixed the CNN feature extraction by introducing the CNN Autoencoder pretraining step, I took responsibility for the ablation study. I designed and ran two experiments to systematically evaluate different architectural choices:

- **LSTM + Attention Autoencoder** — extended the base LSTM model with a Multi-Head Attention mechanism and Layer Normalization to test whether attending over the encoded sequence improves anomaly detection
- **Dense Autoencoder (No Temporal Modeling)** — removed temporal modeling entirely and scored anomalies directly from individual frame reconstruction errors, aggregated into windows, to test the contribution of sequence modeling

For each experiment I handled the full pipeline: model design, training configuration, threshold calibration using Youden's J statistic, and evaluation using ROC AUC, precision, recall, F1 score, and confusion matrices. The experiments revealed that GRU slightly outperforms LSTM with 23% fewer parameters, attention adds modest improvement, and the spatial features from the pretrained CNN are already highly discriminative on their own.

Finally, I was responsible for **editing and finalizing the presentation**. I received the initial draft from my teammate, made significant edits to the structure, content, and figures, and delivered the final polished version of the presentation.
